Case Study

UPocket — Your university in your pocket

Minimal mobile app that lets students at Universidad Panamericana access the essentials instantly — schedule, partial grades and key links — without digging through a slow web portal.

About

Problem & Goal

Problem. The university web portal is slow and requires several security steps just to reach simple tasks like checking today’s class schedule or partial grades — even worse on mobile.

Goal. Build a clear, attractive and easy-to-use mobile app that surfaces the essentials in one place and feels fast on the go.

Role

My Role & Tools

Role. UI/UX Design · Prototyping.

Tools. Figma (Auto-layout, variables, components), Lottie (micro-interactions), Spline (subtle backgrounds). SwiftUI-aware handoff.

Scope & Tasks

What I set out to design

Core Features

  • Home with cards: Today’s Schedule, Grades, Shortcuts.
  • Schedule view with day/week switcher.
  • Grades with partials, “Updated” badge, trend sparkline.

Objectives

  • Reduce steps to top tasks (schedule/grades).
  • Navigation minimal & readable on the go.
  • Consistent system → SwiftUI-friendly specs.

Constraints

  • Started with university brand palette; later moved to a neutral, modern-minimal palette for contrast.
  • High information density → careful layout & type scale.
  • Light motion only; Lottie for micro-delight.

Process

From discovery to final UI

Overview of the steps: discovery, wireframing, prototype, UI design and a small design system to ensure consistency and a smooth SwiftUI handoff.

Research

Student insights

  • Schedule is checked multiple times per day (room changes).
  • Grades spike after each partial; need latest first.
  • Portal feels slow & buried under menus on mobile.
  • Shortcuts (Wi-Fi, payments, map, library) are used often but hard to find.

Personas

Who I designed for

Sofía (19) — needs today’s schedule at a glance; hates deep links.

Carlos (21) — wants partial grades & GPA trend; prefers dark mode.

Ana (22) — needs payments & reminders; likes biometric unlock (concept).

User Flow

Onboarding → Auth (SSO/biometric concept) → Home (Cards: Schedule · Grades · Shortcuts) → Schedule / Grades / More.

Outcome

Results & feedback

  • Faster access to schedule/grades; students described it as “straight to the point”.
  • Visual consistency and motion improved perceived speed.

Learnings

What I’d do next

  • Leverage components/widgets even more.
  • Explore color palettes earlier; collect feedback sooner.
  • Next features: long-press quick actions, push for grade updates, usability testing.
